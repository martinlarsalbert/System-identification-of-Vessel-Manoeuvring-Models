Discrete-time Extended Kalman

4. Discrete-time Extended Kalman¶

\[\dot{x} = f(x,u,w)\]
\[y = h(x,u) + \epsilon\]

Algorithm 4.1 (Discrete-time extended Kalman filter)

Inputs Initial values: \(x_0\), \(P_0\), \(C_d\), \(R_d\), \(Q_d\), \(E_d\)

Output Estimated states: \(\hat{x}\), estimated covariances \(\hat{P}\)

  1. Initial values:

    1. \(\hat{x}^-[0] = x_0\)

    2. \(\hat{P}^-[0] = P_0\)

  2. For \(k\) in \(n\) measurements

    1. KF gain

      1. \(K[k]=\hat{P}^-[k] C_d^T \left(C_d \hat{P}^-[k] C_d^T + R_d\right)^{-1}\)

      2. \(I_{KC} = I_n - K[k] C_d\)

    2. Update

      1. State corrector \(\hat{x}[k] = \hat{x}^-[k] + K[k] eps\) (\(eps = y - C_d \hat{x}^-[k]\))

      2. Covariance corrector \(\hat{P}[k] = I_{KC} \cdot \hat{P}^-[k] I_{KC}^T + K[k] R_d K^T \)

    3. Predict

      1. State predictor \(\hat{x}^-[k+1] = \hat{x}[k] + h \cdot \hat{f}(\hat{x}[k], u[k], w[k])\)

      2. Covariance predictor \(\hat{P}^-[k+1] = A_d \hat{P}[k] A_d^T + E_d Q_d E_d^T \)
